## The target client

Note: This describes Doug's current go-to-market ICP. It is not a permanent product constraint. The platform is industry-agnostic — see CLAUDE.md.

Founder-led B2B consulting or professional services firm.

Qualifying criteria:
  Revenue:          $300K–$3M annually
  Operating tenure: 3+ years in business
  Team size:        Small — founder is still the primary rainmaker
  Pipeline source:  Referral-dependent. No repeatable outbound system.
  Pain:             Growth is capped by the founder's personal network and bandwidth.
                    They know they need outbound. They don't know how to do it well.
                    Or they've tried and got poor results.

Disqualifying signals:
  - Already has a functioning outbound system (no problem to solve)
  - Pre-revenue or under $300K (can't support the service fee)
  - Funded startup with a growth team (wrong buyer, wrong service model)
  - Needs a full marketing agency (MargenticOS is pipeline, not brand)

Buyer persona:
  The founder is the decision-maker and the service recipient.
  They are smart, time-poor, and sceptical of marketing promises.
  They buy on proof — case studies, specific numbers, referrals.
  They do not want to manage another tool or learn a new process.
  They want outcomes: a predictable flow of qualified meetings.

## The offer

Clients receive two things:

### 1. Four living strategy documents (permanent IP)
These are the primary deliverable. The $750 setup fee essentially pays for these.
Clients own them permanently — they keep them even if they leave the service.

  ICP document:         Ideal client profile using JTBD + Four Forces of Progress + Tier model.
                        Not just demographics — motivations, triggers, and switching costs.

  Positioning document: Built on April Dunford's "Obviously Awesome" framework +
                        Geoffrey Moore compression. One clear, defensible position.

  Tone of Voice guide:  Extraction-first — find their real voice from samples, correct bad habits.
                        Never open with I/We, one question max, no feature listing before relevance,
                        first touch under 100 words. The agent improves the voice even when
                        samples show these bad habits.

  Messaging playbook:   Foundation + layer architecture + StoryBrand influence.
                        Covers: cold email, LinkedIn, follow-up, objection handling.

A generic-feeling document is a product failure. Each document must feel like it was
written specifically for this person's business by someone who understands it deeply.
Apply the "AI slop" test rigorously: would a sharp founder cringe reading this?

### 2. Results dashboard
Live view of pipeline activity: meetings booked, reply rates, campaign performance.
Client-facing only — they see results and their strategy documents.
They never see the execution layer (agents, integrations, operator controls).

## Commercial model

### Founding clients (first three)
Setup fee:       $750 (one-time)
Monthly retainer: $500/month for 3 months
In exchange for: Feedback, testimonial, case study

Framing to founding clients:
"You're helping me build this. Your fee is marginal. In return I'll be more
communicative, more iterative, and more involved than any future client will experience."

Founding clients know they're early and have accepted that.
Goal: under-promise and over-deliver on results.

### Ongoing model (post-founding)
To be determined after founding client results are validated.
Do not build billing infrastructure in phase one.

## Service model — what this is NOT

MargenticOS is a services business, not a SaaS product.
Doug runs campaigns manually using existing tools. The system makes that scalable.

This distinction affects every UI and agent decision:
- Clients never see execution controls because they don't need them
- The dashboard is a results and IP display layer, not an operations tool
- Agents don't replace tools — they orchestrate them
- The operator view is Doug's control panel, not an admin interface

When a feature request feels like "letting the client do X themselves," pause and
reconsider. The service model depends on clients not operating the system.
They buy outcomes and IP, not software.

## MargenticOS as client zero

Before any paying client is onboarded, MargenticOS runs as client zero.
Doug fills out the intake questionnaire about MargenticOS.
The four strategy documents are generated for MargenticOS itself.
Live campaigns run for Doug's own pipeline generation.

This is not a test environment. It is a live production run.
Phase one is not complete until client zero has run live campaigns.
The case study from client zero becomes the primary sales asset for acquiring founding clients.
